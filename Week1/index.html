Seasons of New England

Winter

What Is Winter Weather?

Winter weather is classifed as a temperature extreme. The average temperature for New England during winter ranges from 32 degrees fahrenheit to -20 degrees fahrenheit. Geographic locations with higher elevations, particular within the northern region of New England, may experience even lower than average winter temperatures.

What Does Winter Look Like?

Winter has a tendency to make a given area appear mostly cloudy. Most plant-life cannot survive in the cold so they either die or go into dormancy. Because winter is so cold it has the ability to cover areas with clear ice or white snow.

What Is Unique About Winter?

No two snowflakes are alike because each cystal forms differently depending on the weather coniditions and its journey down from the clouds. During the winter season the earth is closest to the sun. Each winter more than 22 million tons of salt are used on US highways, sidewalks and roads.

Spring

What Is Spring Weather?

Spring weather is a transition from colder temperatures to a gradual rise of warm temperatures. It is the season between winter and summer. The average New England spring day-time temperatures range from 51 degrees fahrenheit to 59 degrees fahrenheit.

What Does Spring Look Like?

One should expect to see outside budding and blossoming plants outside. One should also see melting snow and more rainy days. Moreover, the appearance of pollen is more prevalent as wind has a tendency to spread it in the air.

What Is Unique About Spring?

On the first day of spring there is exactly 12 hours of darkeness and 12 hours os daylight. In Old English the season of Spring used to be called "Lent" the season was not called "Spring" until the 14th century. There are less tornadoes in the winter than there are in the spring.

Summer

What Is Summer Weather?

Summer may be considered a temperature extreme. Weather conditions tend to be more hot and dry or humid. The average summer temperatures for New England range from 80 degrees fahrenheit to 90 degrees fahrenheit.

What Does Summer Look Like?

Expect more sunny days and clear blue skies during the summer season. Most plants have fully blossomed and bloomed and animals such as squirrels are no longer in hibernation. If humidity is high there may be sightings of thunderstorms and rain.

What Is Unique About Summer?

The summer solstice marks the longest day of year. Every summer the Eiffel Tower grows an additional 6 inches in size due heat expanding the iron. The month of June was named after the Roman goddess Juno.

Fall

What Is Fall Weather?

Fall weather is the transition from warmer temperatures to a gradual decrese to colder temperatures. It is the season between summer and winter. The average New England fall temperatures range from 70 degrees fahrenheit to 50 degrees fahrenheit.

What Does Fall Look Like?

During the fall season there is autumn foliage which consist of an array of colors from many trees and plants. There is also a migration of birds, so one may see more birds in the sky as the travel south to warmer climates. One will also see fruits and plant-life foraging, flowering, and seeds or leaves falling from them.

What Is Unique About Fall?

The Fall season used to be known as "harvest" until the year 1500. The "harvest moon" was considered to be the fullest moon closest to the date of the autumn equinox. The "aurora borealis" also known as the "Northern Lights" are most likey to occur during the fall and winter months.
